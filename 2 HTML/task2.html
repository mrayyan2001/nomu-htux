History

The oldest known evidence of hominids in Jordan dates back to the Lower Paleolithic period, with the discovery of stone tools and artifacts in various archaeological sites. The region has been inhabited by various civilizations throughout history, including the Ammonites, Moabites, Edomites, Nabataeans, and Romans.

Geography

Jordan is located in the Middle East, bordered by Saudi Arabia to the south and east, Iraq to the northeast.

Climate

Jordan has a Mediterranean climate, with hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. The country experiences significant temperature variations between day and night, especially in desert areas.

Ecology

Jordan is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, with several protected areas and nature reserves. The country has a variety of ecosystems, including deserts, mountains, and wetlands.

Transportation

Jordan has a well-developed transportation network, including roads, railways, and airports. The country is served by Queen Alia International Airport in Amman, which connects Jordan to various international destinations.
